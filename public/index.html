Sigma Nu Fraternity places a strong emphasis on the concept of honor, and its members are expected to uphold the highest standards of integrity and personal conduct. The fraternity's motto, "Love, Truth, and Honor," reflects the importance that it places on these values, and its members strive to embody these principles in all aspects of their lives.

We place a strong emphasis on the recruitment process, also known as "rush," as it is an opportunity for the fraternity to find high-quality men who embody the values and ideals of the organization. The fraternity looks for men who are academically driven, leaders on campus, and have a strong sense of personal integrity. Rush is an important time for both the fraternity and potential new members, as it allows both parties to get to know one another and determine if they are a good fit.

We uphold a strong track record of academic and athletic achievement. In recent years, the fraternity has consistently had one of the highest grade point averages on campus, and many of its members have gone on to distinguished careers in a wide variety of fields.

Our rush process is an important part of the fraternity's recruitment and membership selection process. The goal of rush is to meet a diverse group of high-quality candidates who share the values and goals of the fraternity. By hosting events and activities during the rush period, we aim to give potential new members a chance to get to know the fraternity and its members, while also allowing the fraternity to get to know the potential new members. The goal is to find candidates who are not only academically successful and involved in their communities, but also individuals who share the fraternity's commitment to personal and moral development. Ultimately, the rush process serves as a way for us to identify and recruit new members who will contribute to the positive culture and success of the fraternity.

We typically conducts its rush process exclusively in the fall semester due to the extensive activation process that follows. Activation refers to the process of transitioning a new member from a pledge to a full member of the fraternity. This process typically involves a period of education and training on the values, history, and expectations of the fraternity. By rushing exclusively in the fall, we are able to fully support and guide its new members through the activation process, setting them up for success as active members of the fraternity.
